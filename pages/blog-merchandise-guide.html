How to Order Branded Merchandise That Actually Gets Used

JP
Janta Prints Team
Kisumu, Kenya · Jan 2026
🎁

Most branded merchandise ends up in a drawer within a week. A branded USB drive that doesn't work. A mug with a logo that fades after three washes. A notebook that falls apart. Done wrong, corporate gifts waste money and create negative impressions. Done right, they're walking advertisements that people genuinely use every day.

Start with utility

The most powerful filter for choosing branded merchandise is simple: will the person actually use this? Not "will they be politely grateful" — will they pick it up off their desk tomorrow morning? Items that score high on daily utility:

  • Water bottles and travel tumblers — used daily, visible everywhere
  • Notebooks — used in meetings, on desks, photographed for social media
  • Canvas tote bags — used for shopping, carried to work
  • Good quality pens — always needed, rarely supplied
  • Phone stands and cable organisers — constant desktop presence

What doesn't work

Items that consistently end up unused: cheap key rings, stress balls, generic USB drives (capacity too small to be useful), branded mouse pads (people use laptop trackpads), generic caps with logos that are too large or poorly placed.

The logo size rule: Less is almost always more on branded merchandise. A small, well-placed logo reads as premium. A logo that covers the entire surface of a mug reads as a promotional giveaway. Ask yourself: would someone buy this at full retail price if it had no branding? If yes, your branded version will be used. If no, rethink.

Quality signals that matter

For branded apparel: the weight and feel of the fabric matters more than the print. A 200gsm t-shirt with great printing feels like a real garment. A 140gsm shirt with the same print feels cheap — and the brand on it takes on that association.

For drinkware: a leaking lid or thin ceramic destroys the brand impression. Spend the extra KES 200 per unit on a well-made tumbler. If someone uses it daily for a year, your cost-per-impression is negligible.

Corporate gift packaging

The unboxing experience matters. A branded tote containing a notebook, pen, and tumbler — wrapped in branded tissue paper — costs perhaps KES 200 more per kit in packaging, but is three times as likely to be photographed and shared on social media. It signals that the recipient was worth the effort.
