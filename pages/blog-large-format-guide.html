Large Format Printing: A Guide to Outdoor Advertising in Kenya

JP
Janta Prints Team
Kisumu, Kenya · Feb 2026
🏟️

Large format printing covers everything bigger than A0 — banners, billboards, building wraps, vehicle graphics, trade show displays, and more. It's one of the highest-impact marketing channels available, especially in Kenya's outdoor environment. This guide covers everything you need to know.

What counts as large format?

Anything printed on a wide-format printer — typically machines printing 1.2m, 1.6m, 2.4m, or 3.2m wide. Common large-format products include: roll-up / pull-up banners, PVC banners and mesh banners, building and wall graphics, billboards, vehicle wraps, outdoor posters, and event backdrops.

File specifications for large format

The biggest misconception about large-format printing is that you need huge files. You don't — because large-format prints are viewed from a distance. Here's the practical guide:

  • Small pull-up banner (85×200cm, viewed from 0.5–1m): 100–150 DPI at actual size
  • Outdoor banner (3m×1m, viewed from 2–5m): 72–100 DPI at actual size
  • Billboard (6m×3m, viewed from 10m+): 25–50 DPI at actual size
  • Vehicle wrap panels: 100 DPI at actual size

A practical alternative: design at 1/10th of the actual size at 300 DPI. So a 100cm×200cm banner becomes a 10cm×20cm file at 300 DPI. This gives the same effective resolution and a much more manageable file.

Materials: vinyl, mesh, fabric

PVC Vinyl is the most common outdoor material — durable, waterproof, suitable for banners, billboards, and outdoor displays. It can be reinforced with eyelets for hanging.

Mesh vinyl has small perforations that allow wind to pass through — essential for large outdoor banners and building wraps where wind loading would otherwise tear a solid banner. It's also semi-transparent when viewed up close.

Tension fabric (SEG — Silicone Edge Graphics) is used indoors for backlit displays, trade show walls, and exhibition displays. It looks premium, is lightweight, and can be washed and reused.

For events and trade shows in Kisumu: Pull-up banners and fabric tension displays are the most reusable investment. One good roll-up banner used at 10 events costs far less per impression than 10 disposable paper posters.

Installation and finishing

Most large-format prints need finishing — eyelets for hanging, welded edges for outdoor banners, frame profiles for fabric displays. Tell us how you plan to display the print and we'll advise on the right finishing.

Turnaround times at Janta Prints

  • Pull-up / roll-up banners: same day or next day (submitted before 10am)
  • PVC outdoor banners (up to 3m): 1–2 days
  • Building wrap / billboard: 3–7 days depending on size
  • Vehicle wrap: 2–3 days for print, plus installation
