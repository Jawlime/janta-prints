The Janta Prints Creative Studio: 8 Free Tools for Your Artwork

JP
Janta Prints Team
Kisumu, Kenya · Jan 2026
🎨

The Janta Prints Creative Studio is a free, browser-based image toolkit — no account needed, files never stored permanently. It's designed to help you prepare artwork before ordering, check your files, and visualise how your design will look on a product. Here's a walkthrough of every tool.

1. Background Remover

Upload a photo or logo and the tool removes the background automatically using a Python deep-learning model (U2-Net). No green screen, no manual selection. You can output a transparent PNG (best for logos), a white-background version (for print documents), or a custom colour background.

When to use it: Before submitting a logo or product photo for jersey placement, business card design, or mockup generation. A transparent background gives our production team maximum flexibility.

2. Filters

Twelve photo filters — Vivid, Matte, Vintage, Chrome, Fade, Sharpen, Soft Glow, Dramatic, Warm Sunset, Cool Blue, Sepia, and Original. Each has an intensity slider. Use it to give your product photos a consistent look before adding them to brochures or social media graphics.

3. Crop & Resize

Resize any image to exact print dimensions with preset sizes built in: Business Card (85×55mm), A5 Flyer, A4 Poster, Square (1:1), T-Shirt Front (30×40cm), Jersey (25×30cm), Pull-up Banner ratio, and Custom. Set the output DPI (72 for screen, 150–300 for print). Lock aspect ratio or resize freely.

Pro tip: Use the 300 DPI preset for any artwork going to print. If your file comes out small at 300 DPI, your original image is too low-resolution — and now you know before we discover it during production.

Not a substitute for professional artwork preparation. The Studio is a quick-fix tool for common tasks. For professional print work — especially CMYK colour matching, bleed setup, and multi-layer artwork — you still need a proper design application like Illustrator, InDesign, or Photoshop.

4. Colour Adjustments

Eight sliders: Brightness, Contrast, Saturation, Sharpness, Hue Shift, Highlights, Shadows, and Temperature. Adjust photos to match your brand colours before printing. The Temperature slider shifts the image warm or cool — useful for correcting mixed-lighting photos.

5. B&W Converter

Four modes: smooth Greyscale (best for photos), hard Threshold (best for logos and line art), Adaptive Threshold (smart local thresholding for mixed content), and High-Contrast B&W (CLAHE enhancement for dark or flat images). Useful for artwork that will print in single-colour spot black, or for creating a clean version of a logo for embroidery.

6. Text & Watermark

Add text or a tiled watermark to any image. Choose from four fonts (Impact, Bold Sans, Georgia, Courier), set size, colour, opacity, position (9-point grid), and rotation. Enable "Tile as watermark" to repeat the text diagonally across the entire image — useful for creating sample or proof images that can't be used without ordering.

7. Format Converter

Convert between JPEG, PNG, WebP, TIFF, BMP, and PDF. Set JPEG/WebP quality. Use the batch converter to convert up to 10 files at once and download as a ZIP. Useful for getting files into the right format for print submission.

8. Mockup Generator

Place your design onto 11 product templates: T-Shirt Front/Back, Football Kit, Rugby Kit, Polo, Hoodie, Cap, Tote Bag, Mug, Banner, and A4 Flyer. Choose from 8 colour variants. The backend uses OpenCV perspective transform to place your design realistically on the product. This is how to visualise your jersey or t-shirt design before committing to production.

Best results: Remove the background from your design first (Tool 1), then use the Mockup Generator. A transparent-background design placed on the product looks far more realistic than a white-background one.
