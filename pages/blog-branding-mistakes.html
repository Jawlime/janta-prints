5 Branding Mistakes Kisumu Businesses Make (And How to Fix Them)

JP
Janta Prints Team
Kisumu, Kenya · Feb 2026
🎨

We see the same branding mistakes repeatedly from Kenyan businesses — most of them invisible to the business owner, but immediately obvious to a print professional. Here are the five most common, and exactly how to fix them.

1. Using a low-resolution logo everywhere

The most common mistake by far. A logo that looks fine as a small icon on a website will print blurry on a business card, banner, or jersey. If you're using a JPEG or PNG that's smaller than 500×500px, your logo is low-resolution.

Fix: Get your logo in SVG or AI (vector) format from your designer. Vectors are resolution-independent — they stay sharp at any size. If you only have a raster file, contact us and we can advise on whether it's usable at your print size.

2. Inconsistent colours across print jobs

Your brand has specific Pantone or CMYK colour values — but if you're not specifying them, every vendor will print slightly different versions of "your blue" or "your red." Over time, your business cards, banners, and signage start to look like they're from different companies.

Fix: Define your exact CMYK values and put them in a simple brand guide. For example: Primary Blue = C:90 M:65 Y:0 K:0. Share this with every print vendor you work with, including Janta Prints.

RGB ≠ CMYK. Your web designer may give you RGB values like #0033CC. That's a screen colour — convert it to CMYK before giving it to a printer. The exact conversion: Image → Mode → CMYK in Photoshop, or use an online converter. Note that the CMYK equivalent may look slightly different.

3. Using transparent PNG backgrounds incorrectly

A logo on a transparent PNG background looks great on a white slide — but when printed on a coloured background (a banner, a coloured t-shirt, a dark board), the logo might have a white box around it, or the transparent area might print as white or grey.

Fix: For print, always ask your designer for a version of the logo specifically set up for the background colour you're printing on. A white logo on a transparent background for dark substrates; a coloured logo for light substrates.

4. Too many fonts and colours

Using 4+ fonts and 5+ colours on a flyer is the fastest way to make your business look unestablished. A strong brand uses 1–2 typefaces and 2–3 colours consistently across everything.

Fix: Pick one display font for headings and one body font. Pick a primary brand colour, a secondary, and a neutral (usually black or white). Everything else is detail.

5. Not keeping your original files

We meet clients regularly who have lost their original brand files — the AI file for their logo, the PSD for their flyer. They only have a screenshot or a small WhatsApp image. Starting over is expensive.

Fix: Back up all original design files to Google Drive or Dropbox. Store them in a folder called "Brand Assets" and share access with a trusted team member. Never rely on your designer having them — once you've paid for artwork, the files are yours.
